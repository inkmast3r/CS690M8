## A Day in the Life of Daniel

Morning

Daniel gets into his car and notices the fuel gauge is low. “Didn’t I just fill up last week?” he wonders. He can’t remember how much he spent or how long his last tank lasted. “It would be nice to track my fuel costs,” he thinks.

Late Morning

While driving to work, Daniel hears a strange noise from the engine. “When was the last time I got an oil change?” he asks himself. He flips through old receipts in the glovebox but can’t find the answer.

Lunch

At lunch, Daniel talks to a friend about car expenses. “I track my maintenance schedule,” his friend says. “It helps me avoid unexpected repairs.” Daniel realizes that his car is due for a tire rotation soon but has no system to remind him.

Afternoon

Later in the day, Daniel receives a notification from his insurance company about renewal. “I should probably check if my last inspection is still valid,” he thinks, unsure of when he last had it done.

Evening

As Daniel winds down for the day, he thinks about how much he relies on his car but feels unprepared when it comes to maintenance and expenses. “If I had a tool to log fuel usage, track service dates, and remind me about maintenance, I’d feel more in control,” he resolves.


## Running the application:

- Download the executable

- Go to the latest release: v3.0.0

- Download the executable called CarCareTracker.zip

- Unzip it in your local




- Install dotnet runtime .NET Runtime
  
- Go inside the directory you have unzipped
  
- Run the following command in terminal:  `dotnet CarCareTracker.dll`
